About

"...everyone should learn how to program a computer, because it teaches you how to think..." -Steve Jobs

“Talk is cheap. Show me the code.”― Linus Torvalds

“Computer Science and Engineering” is all the rage these days. A lot of students are now pursuing the dream of being a successful programmer, developer, software engineer and so on. Some of us are fueled by passion, some by the success stories, some just want to make a game and others do not know how to negotiate with their parents. Whatever the reason, a lot of us are now going through the semesters learning algorithms, designing logic and of course debugging. However, in the end only a handful would qualify for a proper job or even pursue a programming career. This is because we are so caught up in curriculum that we ignore the value of working for ourselves, of creating something new, of programming for the sake of more than ourselves.

And so we have decided that we shall celebrate the joy of programming by collaborating in group projects to not only showcase our talents but also to build a platform to engage in social learning and development. We plan to utilize whatever skill we have at our disposal to make something entirely for ourselves. We are not doing this to impress our faculties; we are doing this to impress ourselves. We want to someday look back at this little gesture and smile. And we hope that by doing this we can ensure a bright future for ourselves.
